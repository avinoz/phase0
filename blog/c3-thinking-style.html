My Quirkiness in Learning

Ways I Learn Best

07/27/15

I’m not that old (I think?) but it’s been about 15 years since I’ve last had to read textbooks and pass tests. I studied architecture in college and that was a whole different ball game when it comes to learning styles. It was a much more hands on experience with abstract concepts but very immediate and tangible outcomes. It was also never a right or wrong answer; more like if it doesn’t please the crowd, then go back and make a more valid argument. It has occurred to me through the last three weeks that I needed to re-visit the way I learn. I know I’m a visual and tactile person so reading words on the computer has proven to be very difficult for me to get into my head. I’ve started jotting down notes with pen to paper and it’s helped me digest the material that I’ve been reading. I’m still having a hard time reading technical data. I have no problem finding it on the web but being able to process and digest it has been hard. Partially because there are many ways to do something and countless short-hands which I’ve just had to accept until I read up on what it symbolizes. This coming week I will try a new method whereby I will print out some webpages for me to do some highlighting and take some more time off-screen (sorry trees!) I’m also going to try and pair more, and join some of the tutor lessons where I might be able to get some more simplified answers. Well fingers-crossed; I’ll keep you guys updated to how things go!
