Sterotype Threat

08/14/15

I get it, judging a book by it’s cover. It’s a way that people deal with the world around them. I don’t think that it’s wrong to stereotype when meeting new people as long as these remain as first impressions. I firmly believe in image and identity; we dress and act the way that we want to be seen in the world. Whether or not we like it, we all have negative astigmatisms associated to us either culturally, racially or otherwise but I believe that the problem rises when people hold others to those assumptions and without the possibility of change.

There is a lot of un-just in the world we live in and I’ve definitely had my share. I don’t generally express my political opinions but since we’re on this topic, I’d like to share my views on racism against my Asian culture. Yes you heard right, black people are not the only ones who experience stereotype threats. Let’s not forget that Asians were brought to America as slaves as well. Our race was also denied jobs etc and yet we pulled ourselves together and started ground up creating a new industry for ourselves as restaurateurs. We even bonded together after that and formed alliances to help others learn the skill of cooking American Chinese food which lead spread all across the country.

What I see these days is that other minority groups always complain about racism and yet Asians are forgotten, and especially under-represented in the media. Look at all the actors, you can count the number of Asians in the industry. Just because we excel with hard work doesn’t mean that we have no prejudice against us. Look, we don't sit around pointing fingers and complaining that we have a bad hand.

Think about it; til next week. Feels good to get that off my chest :)
