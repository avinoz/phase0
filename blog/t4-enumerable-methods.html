Two Useful Methods

Well.. It's Really Just One With Two Names!

08/03/15

I tried to spin this into a funny post but sadly I’m out of juice right now. So, clear cut and dry it is! The map method! What does it do? Well, it plows through an array and look for what you ask it to and put them into a new array. Cool eh? It’s a very nice time saver. Before I jump into examples, I want to point out that #map is synonymous to #collect. Yup, you say potato, I say pot-at-o!

Alright and here are a few examples that will help shape your understanding:

(1..4).map {|i| i*i}
#=> [1, 4, 9, 16]

So what’s this saying? It asks ruby to take all the numbers between 1 and 4 and put each one of them it into this equation where the number is represented by the variable “i” and stick them all in a new array. Easy right? If you don’t give it a block with an equation, it’ll just return an enumerator.
