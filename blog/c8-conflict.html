08/28/15

The last truly upsetting conflict that I had was with my ex-roommates; there were three of us living in a house and I felt that I was the one managing the whole house. When the toilet paper ran out, the empty roller would sit in the holder and a new roll would be set on the floor next to the toilet. If we ran out of something fingers would be pointed as to who purchased something last. I felt I was living with teenagers. I felt that my kindness was taken advantage of without even an appreciation in return.

We had several open conversations about our situation with participation and consideration for others in the household but I think our values were too miss-matched. We tried mending the situation by hiring the maid more frequently, trying to share meals together but it was a lost cause and we parted ways.

Iâ€™d never thought that a roommate situation could have caused such long-lasting emotional turmoil. If I could go back I would apply my skills for kind feedback and hope they would do the same in return; a lot of negative emotions and words were exchanged and eventually broke any friendship that was left. Through this whole fiasco, I learned a lot about my current stage in life and the type of relationships that I want surrounding me. Sometimes parting ways early is wise.
