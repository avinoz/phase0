Box Model Anyone?

Margins, Borders & Padding

07/20/15

This is a diagram of what we call the box model for CSS.

As you can see, there are a few layers to this concept. First you have the content that is in middle defined and confined by the height and width creating the inner dotted box. If there are no more added parameters, this is what you'll see show up on your site.

Padding is space that your content will not bleed into but shares the same traits as your content such as background color. this is the area that is shown in mint green.

Most people think of a border being a line around stuff and that's just what it is! Border surrounds the content and padding. It can be defined in terms of thickness, solid or dashed and color. In this diagram it's the thick black line. You will notice that you can address each side of the content separately.

Margins are the last thing on this diagram.It pretty much gives you a buffer against the next object. It exists after the border and has no background values to it meaning it's invisible space!
